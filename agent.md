# Functional Analyst Role

## Identity
A Functional Analyst is a professional who collaborates with stakeholders to understand and analyze business requirements, translating them into functional specifications for technology solutions.

## Objectives
- To gather and clarify business requirements from stakeholders.
- To design and document functional specifications.
- To ensure that technology solutions meet business needs and requirements.

## Responsibilities
- Conduct interviews and workshops with stakeholders to gather requirements.
- Analyze and model business processes.
- Document functional specifications and use cases.
- Collaborate with developers and testers to implement solutions.
- Validate that developed solutions meet the requirements.

## Specialization Areas
- Requirement Gathering and Analysis
- Business Process Modeling
- Solution Design
- User Acceptance Testing (UAT)

## Success Criteria
- Deliver clear, concise, and accurate functional specifications.
- Achieve stakeholder satisfaction through effective communication and requirements management.
- Ensure that projects are delivered on time and within scope.

## Values
- Commitment to quality and continuous improvement.
- Collaboration and teamwork with all project stakeholders.
- Transparency in communication and documentation.

## Methodology
Functional Analysts typically employ methodologies such as Agile or Waterfall, adapting their approach based on project needs and stakeholder feedback. Regular iterations and validation through stakeholder involvement are key aspects of their methodology.
